# Tour Planner - B2C Business Model

## Executive Summary

Tour Planner's B2C model is a consumer travel planning and booking platform focused on cruise passengers, vacation travelers, families, and small groups. The consumer product combines itinerary capture, excursion discovery, collaborative planning, budget management, and AI-powered recommendations inside a single travel workspace. Revenue is driven primarily by excursion commissions, consumer trip planning subscriptions, premium AI features, affiliate revenue, and sponsored placements.

### Relationship to the Overall Platform

The B2C business is the self-serve demand engine for the overall Tour Planner platform. It brings travelers into the ecosystem through direct trip planning, booking, and sharing. The B2B business extends the same core platform into managed travel use cases where coordinators, agencies, and enterprise operators organize trips on behalf of larger groups. In practice:

- **B2C** drives traveler acquisition, excursion demand, and viral growth through itinerary sharing.
- **B2B** monetizes operational coordination, governance, and managed group workflows.
- Both models share the same product foundation: trip intake, itinerary management, AI planning, budget tools, and booking infrastructure.

## Platform Overview

### Core Capabilities

The B2C product combines several consumer-facing features:

- **Shore Excursion Marketplace** - Curated excursion discovery and booking
- **Trip Intake Engine** - Structured collection of flight, cruise, hotel, and car details
- **Collaborative Trip Planning** - Real-time shared itinerary building with conflict detection
- **Shareable Itineraries** - Share with friends and family using view-only or edit permissions
- **AI-Powered Itinerary Assistant** - AgentCore integration for smart scheduling and recommendations
- **Budget & Cost Management** - Real-time expense tracking with cost optimization recommendations
- **Affiliate Integrations** - Dining reservations and shopping commission networks
- **Live Schedule Updates** - Real-time synchronization with cruise and flight schedules

### Target Customers

1. **Cruise Passengers** - Primary market for port-day planning and excursion booking
2. **Vacation Travelers** - Multi-destination leisure travelers
3. **Families and Friends** - Small groups coordinating shared itineraries
4. **Celebration Travel Groups** - Reunions, destination birthdays, and weddings on the consumer side

## Primary Revenue Streams

### 1. Shore Excursion Marketplace Commission

**Model:** Marketplace commission on excursion bookings

**Revenue Mechanism:**
- Vendors list excursions on the platform
- Customers discover and book via integrated itinerary interface
- Platform earns commission on completed transactions

**Commission Structure:**
- **Average Commission:** 20–30% of booking value
- **Example:** $200 excursion booking → $40–$60 platform revenue

**Vendor Value Proposition:**
- Increased visibility to cruise passengers with port-specific intelligence
- Intelligent scheduling aligned with ship arrival/departure times
- Higher conversion through integrated itinerary planning
- Access to group booking aggregation

**Projected Revenue Contribution:** 50–60% of total revenue

---

### 2. Trip Planning Subscriptions (Freemium Model)

**Model:** Tiered subscription pricing based on trip complexity and collaborators

#### Free Tier
- 1 free trip plan
- Up to 3 travelers
- Basic itinerary builder
- Excursion booking capabilities
- Share itineraries with view-only access to 5 contacts
- Real-time notifications for changes made by collaborators

#### Pro Trip Planner
**Price:** $8–$15 per trip
- Unlimited edits and revisions
- Up to 10 travelers
- **Full sharing capabilities:** Share with up to 25 contacts with editable or view-only roles
- Real-time collaborative editing with change notifications
- Smart scheduling with conflict detection
- Destination-specific recommendations
- Schedule optimization
- Shareable public link (with unique token) for easy distribution

#### Group Planner
**Price:** $25–$50 per trip
- Up to 20+ participants
- **Social sharing features:** Public shareable itinerary link for easy friend/family invitations
- Collaborative editing with role management (editor, viewer, voter)
- Activity voting and group consensus
- Expense splitting and cost allocation
- Integrated group chat with @mentions
- Automatic invitation system with email notifications

#### Extended Group Planner
**Price:** $75-$150 per trip
- Larger leisure groups (20-50 participants)
- Centralized group itinerary workspace
- Shared excursion planning and voting
- Group-wide trip updates and notifications
- Shared budget visibility and split-cost planning

#### Collaboration-Based Dynamic Pricing
Pricing scales based on the number of active collaborators editing the trip:
- **1–3 collaborators:** Free
- **4–6 collaborators:** $12
- **7–12 collaborators:** $25
- **12+ collaborators:** $50

This model encourages viral adoption (users invite friends via shareable links) while monetizing complex group coordination. Each new collaborator invited via shared itinerary represents potential upsell to higher tiers.

**Projected Revenue Contribution:** 10–15% of total revenue

### 7. Dynamic Pricing Premium Services

**Model:** Revenue uplift through intelligent demand-based pricing

**Mechanism:**
- Platform analyzes booking demand, weather, port congestion, seasonality
- Suggests optimized pricing to vendors
- Platform retains percentage of incremental revenue

**Example Scenario:**
- Base excursion price: $100
- Dynamic optimized price: $120 (high season, high demand)
- Revenue split:
  - Vendor receives base price ($100)
  - Additional $20 shared 50/50 → $10 to vendor, $10 to platform
  - Platform commission (20%) on base: $20
  - **Total platform revenue per booking:** $30 ($20 base commission + $10 dynamic share)
  - **Vendor net:** $110 (vs $80 with static pricing)

**Projected Revenue Contribution:** 3–5% of total revenue (embedded in marketplace commission)

### 8. Viral Growth Through Shareable Itineraries

**Model:** Network effects driven by social sharing and collaboration mechanics

**Mechanism:**

**Individual Sharing (Friends & Family):**
- Users create itinerary → Share shareable link with friends and family
- Shared contacts can view, comment, add suggestions (editable or view-only)
- Each collaborator invited = potential new paid user acquisition
- Free users upgrade to Pro ($8–$15) to unlock full sharing capabilities
- Group sharing creates natural upgrade path: 4+ collaborators → Group Planner tier ($25–$50)

**Consumer Group Sharing:**
- Trip organizer creates master itinerary for family or friend group
- Shares with invited travelers who can comment, vote, or edit
- Shared planning increases engagement and drives group-tier upgrades
- Every collaborator invited creates low-cost organic user acquisition

**Network Effects:**
- Each shared itinerary = invitation to platform (user acquisition)
- Shared trips generate 3–5x more collaborators than solo planning
- Collaborators upgrade at higher rate (60%+ conversion vs 15–20% organic)
- Average user (Year 3) reaches 12–15 collaborators over lifetime
- Viral coefficient: 1.5–2.0 (each paid user generates 1.5–2 new users)

**Revenue Impact:**
- **Acquisition:** Reduce CAC by 40–50% vs paid marketing
- **Retention:** Shared trips have 3x higher engagement
- **Expansion:** Collaborators upsell into higher tiers
- **Monetization:** Shareable teams pay for Group/Event tiers

**Projected Revenue Contribution:** 15–20% attributed revenue (user acquisition cost reduction)

### Roll-Up Assumptions for Combined Company Model

The B2C forecast is intended to roll into a combined company model alongside the B2B forecast. To keep the roll-up defensible, these assumptions should be applied consistently:

- **B2C revenue includes only self-serve consumer revenue** from direct travelers and leisure groups.
- **B2B-managed bookings are excluded from B2C totals** even if the underlying traveler uses the same itinerary or booking infrastructure.
- **Vendor SaaS revenue should be assigned once** in the combined model based on the primary commercial owner of the account.
- **Shared product infrastructure costs are not allocated here**; they should be modeled centrally in the consolidated operating model.
- **Consumer GMV should remain separate from B2B managed traveler volume** so marketplace scale is visible without double counting.
- **Cross-segment conversion should be tracked as a pipeline effect, not duplicated as revenue**. For example, a traveler acquired through B2C who later joins a B2B-managed event should count once in revenue and separately in attribution.

**Target:** Cruise passengers at major ports with emphasis on viral sharing mechanics
- Focus on Caribbean cruise routes
- Partner with 2–3 major cruise lines for beta access

**Channels:**
- Cruise line partnerships and app integrations
- Onboard promotions and crew training
