# Tour Planner - Executive Summary

Tour Planner is building a travel coordination and commerce platform that unifies itinerary planning, collaboration, group management, booking, and AI-assisted optimization in a single system. The company serves both self-serve travelers and managed travel organizations, creating a business that combines the scale potential of a travel marketplace with the durability of SaaS and enterprise workflow software.

The core opportunity exists because travel planning remains fragmented. Travelers still coordinate trips through emails, spreadsheets, booking confirmations, chat threads, and disconnected reservation tools. Families and small groups struggle to keep plans aligned. Coordinators and event managers still manage high-value group travel with manual processes. Vendors and enterprise partners lack a clean system that connects itinerary intent, group coordination, and monetizable booking activity.

Tour Planner solves this by becoming the operating layer between trip intent and trip execution. On the consumer side, the platform supports trip intake, shareable itineraries, collaborative planning, budget management, excursion discovery, and AI-generated optimization. On the business side, it supports coordinator-led travel management, group approvals, shared budgets, role-based administration, supplier workflows, and enterprise integrations. Detailed feature and functionality definitions remain in [b2c-model.md](b2c-model.md) and [b2b-model.md](b2b-model.md).

The business model is intentionally diversified. Consumer revenue is generated through excursion and activity commissions, premium trip planning subscriptions, AI concierge upgrades, affiliate reservations, and advertising. Business revenue comes from enterprise SaaS subscriptions, coordinator seat licenses, managed-trip platform fees, group booking commissions, integration services, analytics modules, and supplier tooling. This creates multiple monetization events across a single trip lifecycle and reduces dependence on any one revenue stream.

The strategic value of Tour Planner comes from the fact that it sits earlier than the booking itself. The platform understands who is traveling, when they are traveling, where they are going, what they are considering, and how they are coordinating with others. That intent layer makes Tour Planner valuable not only as a direct product, but also as an integration surface for cruise operators, travel management companies, reservation platforms, hospitality groups, procurement systems, and enterprise travel workflows.

The platform is also structurally advantaged by shareable itineraries. Collaboration is not simply a convenience feature; it acts as a distribution mechanism. One traveler can invite multiple collaborators, groups create higher-value monetization opportunities, and enterprise coordinators can bring entire cohorts of travelers onto the platform. This supports lower customer acquisition cost, better retention, and stronger expansion into both consumer and enterprise accounts.

The current combined revenue outlook reflects this two-engine structure. Based on the existing B2C and B2B assumptions, the company projects approximately $10.25M in Year 1 combined revenue, growing to $47.25M in Year 2, $212.0M in Year 3, $693.0M in Year 4, and $1.651B in Year 5. In this structure, B2C drives demand and marketplace scale while B2B adds recurring contract revenue, operational lock-in, and higher-value account expansion.

For investors, Tour Planner represents a category-bridging opportunity across marketplace commerce, collaboration software, AI-driven planning, and enterprise coordination. For corporate and strategic partners, it offers a ready-made integration layer that can extend traveler engagement, group management, supplier orchestration, and monetization without requiring those capabilities to be built from scratch.

Tour Planner's strength is not one isolated feature. Its advantage is the combination of structured trip intent capture, shareable coordination, embedded commerce, AI-assisted planning, and the ability to package the same platform for both consumers and managed travel organizations. That combination gives the company multiple growth levers, multiple revenue layers, and multiple strategic paths to scale.
