# Tour Planner - Investor Deck Outline

## Purpose

This document translates the revenue model pitch into a 12-15 slide investor deck structure. It is intended for fundraising conversations, strategic investor meetings, and board-style presentations.

Supporting detail remains in:

- [b2c-model.md](b2c-model.md)
- [b2b-model.md](b2b-model.md)
- [revenue-model-pitch.md](revenue-model-pitch.md)

---

## Slide 1 - Title

### Title
Tour Planner

### Subtitle
The operating layer for travel planning, group coordination, and excursion commerce

### Key Message
Tour Planner brings itinerary planning, collaboration, booking, and AI optimization into one platform for both consumers and managed travel organizations.

---

## Slide 2 - Problem

### Title
Travel Planning Is Still Fragmented

### Key Points
- Travelers coordinate trips across email, spreadsheets, chat threads, and booking sites.
- Families and groups struggle to keep itineraries, budgets, and decisions aligned.
- Coordinators and agencies still manage high-value group travel with manual workflows.
- Suppliers and partners lack a modern coordination layer tied to actual trip intent.

### Investor Takeaway
The market is large, painful, and structurally underserved by disconnected point solutions.

---

## Slide 3 - Solution

### Title
One Platform for Planning, Coordination, and Commerce

### Key Points
- Capture structured trip intent.
- Coordinate travelers and groups through shareable itineraries.
- Monetize booking decisions, upgrades, and partner activity.
- Extend the same platform to enterprise and managed travel use cases.

### Investor Takeaway
Tour Planner is not just a booking surface. It owns the workflow before, during, and after key travel decisions.

---

## Slide 4 - Product Scope

### Title
Consumer Demand Engine + Enterprise Coordination Layer

### Key Points
- **B2C:** Cruise travelers, families, leisure groups, celebration travel.
- **B2B:** Corporate event coordinators, TMCs, group operators, destination partners.
- Shared core platform: itinerary planning, budgets, AI support, collaboration, booking infrastructure.

### Investor Takeaway
One product foundation serves two monetization models without requiring two separate businesses.

---

## Slide 5 - Why Now

### Title
Market Timing Is Favorable

### Key Points
- Cruise and group travel demand are strong.
- Travelers now expect collaborative, mobile-first planning experiences.
- AI materially improves itinerary generation and optimization.
- Companies are spending again on offsites, incentive trips, and customer events.
- Suppliers need better digital distribution and pricing infrastructure.

### Investor Takeaway
The market has shifted in a way that makes this platform materially more valuable now than even a few years ago.

---

## Slide 6 - Business Model

### Title
Multiple Monetization Layers

### Key Points
- Excursion and activity commissions
- Consumer subscriptions
- Premium AI planning services
- Affiliate and advertising revenue
- Enterprise SaaS subscriptions
- Coordinator seat licenses and managed-trip fees
- Integration, analytics, and supplier tooling revenue

### Investor Takeaway
The business does not rely on a single monetization stream and can compound revenue per trip, per user, and per account.

---

## Slide 7 - B2C Revenue Engine

### Title
Consumer Revenue Scales with Demand and Sharing

### Key Points
- Primary engine: marketplace commissions on excursions and activities.
- Upsell layers: trip planning subscriptions, AI concierge, affiliate commerce.
- Shareable itineraries drive user acquisition and group upgrades.
- Consumer product becomes the entry point for repeat travel behavior.

### Investor Takeaway
B2C drives top-of-funnel demand, trip volume, and organic growth loops.

---

## Slide 8 - B2B Revenue Engine

### Title
Enterprise Revenue Adds Recurrence and Durability

### Key Points
- SaaS subscriptions for coordinator teams and managed travel programs.
- Seat licensing, trip fees, and governance modules expand account value.
- Group booking commissions add transaction upside on top of contracted revenue.
- Integrations and compliance tooling create switching costs.

### Investor Takeaway
B2B improves retention quality, contract value, and defensibility.

---

## Slide 9 - Network Effects and Distribution

### Title
Shareable Itineraries Create Built-In Distribution

### Key Points
- One traveler brings in multiple collaborators.
- Groups create more planning activity and higher-value tiers.
- Managed events bring entire cohorts of travelers into the system.
- Suppliers gain stronger reason to participate when intent and bookings are visible.

### Investor Takeaway
Collaboration is both a feature and a growth channel.

---

## Slide 10 - Market Opportunity

### Title
Large and Expanding Travel Coordination Opportunity

### Key Points
- Large cruise and excursion commerce market.
- Broader leisure travel planning market.
- Corporate and managed-group travel software opportunity.
- Supplier tooling and integration surface expand monetizable TAM.

### Investor Takeaway
This is larger than a single travel niche. Tour Planner sits across marketplace, SaaS, and coordination software categories.

## Slide 12 - Strategic Value for Partners

### Title
An Attractive Integration Layer for Corporations and Platforms

### Key Points
- Connects traveler intent with commerce and coordination.
- Extends enterprise travel, procurement, and booking workflows.
- Enables partner monetization through embedded commerce and preferred supply.
- Reduces time to market versus building itinerary collaboration and group coordination from scratch.

### Investor Takeaway
Tour Planner has strategic value beyond standalone app economics.

---

## Slide 13 - Competitive Advantage

### Title
Harder to Replace Than a Point Solution

### Key Points
- Structured trip intake and planning workflow
- Shareable itineraries and group coordination
- Embedded commerce and supplier monetization
- AI-powered planning and optimization
- Consumer and enterprise packaging on one platform
- Integration surface across travel and procurement ecosystems

### Investor Takeaway
Defensibility comes from workflow ownership, not a single isolated feature.

---

## Slide 14 - Go-to-Market

### Title
Land Through Demand, Expand Through Coordination

### Key Points
- Acquire consumers through travel demand and sharing loops.
- Expand into larger leisure groups and repeat planners.
- Sell B2B through design partners, agencies, coordinators, and operators.
- Grow enterprise value through seats, compliance, integrations, and managed bookings.

### Investor Takeaway
The GTM supports both fast demand generation and higher-value enterprise expansion.

---

## Slide 15 - The Ask / Closing

### Title
Why Tour Planner Wins

### Closing Points
- Tour Planner owns the coordination layer before and around the booking.
- It monetizes both transaction flow and operational workflow.
- It serves consumers, coordinators, and suppliers on one platform.
- It is positioned for both venture-scale growth and strategic integration value.

### Optional Final Ask
- Capital to accelerate product, partnerships, and go-to-market
- Strategic introductions to cruise, travel, and enterprise coordination partners
- Pilot opportunities with integration-minded corporations and operators

---

## Presentation Notes

- Keep the narrative commercial, not technical.
- Use the B2C and B2B documents only as backup material for detailed questions.
- Do not overload the main deck with feature lists; keep the message centered on market, model, growth, and strategic value.
- If presenting to corporate partners instead of investors, emphasize slides 12 through 15 more heavily.
